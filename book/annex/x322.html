If this limit really exists, we may say that the function f(x) is derivable in x0. If we are making the graphical plotting of the function f(x), the derivative represents in a specific point an angular coefficient of the tangent to the curve. It is possible that the limit of this ratio to have two values in a single point, just as tends to zero by means of positive or negative values; we may say that we are dealing with a derivative oriented either to the left or to the right.
